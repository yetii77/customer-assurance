Access Control

Data Access

Access to Vizcom’s backend databases is restricted to essential personnel only, primarily within the engineering and security teams. Access is managed according to the principle of least privilege and is secured through strict Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) and Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).

Logging

System access and activities are logged and monitored.

Password Security

Vizcom’s general user password policy is listed below:

  • Use passwords with at least 12 characters.
  • Block weak or compromised passwords.
  • Use a password manager.
  • Allow special characters, spaces, and emojis.
  • Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA).

Email Protection

Vizcom has implemented an antispam solution in their messaging service to filter out unsolicited and potentially malicious emails. Antispam solutions help reduce the volume of spam emails and protect users from phishing attempts and other email-based threats.

Employee Training

Privacy awareness training is conducted for all new employees upon onboarding, and refreshed annually for all employees. We also extend these obligations to subcontractors or third parties we engage with.

HR Security

Security protocols are integrated into HR processes.

Incident Response

Vizcom has developed detailed incident response protocols that outline the steps our team will take to investigate, mitigate, and resolve any reported harm or adverse impact. We have dedicated contact persons for cybersecurity incidents and established protocols for reporting, responding to, and learning from such incidents. We have a 24x7x365 staffed phone number available to customers and clients for reporting security incidents. The SLA for notification depends on the severity of the incident, with critical incidents requiring notification within 24 hours.

Internal Assessments

Vizcom has a documented governance process to identify and assess any changes that could significantly affect our internal controls for security, confidentiality, and availability. These processes are periodically reviewed and updated by our CTO and security team.

Penetration Testing

Our AI models undergo rigorous adversarial testing, including red teaming and penetration testing, to identify and address vulnerabilities effectively.

Data Privacy

Cookies

Our privacy policies and notices are conspicuously provided to clients in clear and plain language. They identify the purposes for collecting data, categories of data collected, use of outside data sources, affiliates or non-affiliated third parties with whom the data is shared, and include opt-out mechanisms.

Data Breach Notifications

For breaches that directly impact consumer data or systems, Vizcom's breach notification policies include immediate investigation and communication with the affected consumer. We outline the nature of the breach, the category and approximate number of data records concerned, the likely consequences, and the measures taken or proposed to address the breach, including efforts to mitigate possible adverse effects.

Data Security

Access Reviews

Administrative access is tightly controlled through restrictive admin/Active Directory group access, with multi-factor authentication enforced for all remote access. Access logs are regularly reviewed, and access rights are audited to ensure adherence to the principle of least privilege.

Backups Enabled

Vizcom has a process in place to identify critical resources and data within their infrastructure. Identifying critical resources and data enables Vizcom to prioritize their backup and restore efforts effectively.

Data Erasure

Secured data deletion is ensured. Vizcom utilizes cryptographic erasure and is compliant with industry standards.

Encryption-at-rest

Encryption keys are encrypted at rest and when transmitted. This ensures that even if a key is intercepted during transfer or storage, it cannot be used without first being decrypted.

Encryption-in-transit

Data at rest is encrypted using AES-256 encryption standards. Data in transit is secured using TLS 1.2 or higher to ensure secure data transfer.

Physical Security

Vizcom’s Physical Security PolicQy outlines all measures and protocols to secure physical assets. The policy covers all aspects of physical security, including access control and surveillance.

Endpoint Security

Disk Encryption

Vizcom ensures that endpoints' hard drives are encrypted using hardware-based encryption methods, such as full-disk encryption or self-encrypting drives. Encrypting the hard drives of endpoints provides an additional layer of protection for data stored on them, even if the devices are lost or stolen. It is monitored via our MSM platform for compliance.

DNS Filtering

DNS Filtering is implemented.

Endpoint Detection and Response

Vizcom has implemented an Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) solution on all endpoints within their infrastructure. EDR solutions provide advanced threat detection and response capabilities, enhancing Vizcom's overall security posture.

Threat Detection

Proactive threat detection mechanisms and processes are employed to identify and respond to potential security threats. This includes security information and event management (SIEM) systems, intrusion detection/prevention systems, and anomaly detection.

Infrastructure

AWS

Vizcom deploys services within Amazon Web Services infrastructure. AWS physical infrastructure provides a higher level of isolation and security, minimizing the risk of unauthorized access and potential attacks.

Anti-DDoS

Measures in place to protect against Distributed Denial of Service attacks. These measures include traffic monitoring and filtering to mitigate potential disruptions.

BC/DR

Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery plans are established and regularly tested to ensure minimal disruption to services in the event of an incident. These plans include redundant systems, data backups, and failover procedures.

Infrastructure Security

Security best practices are applied to the underlying infrastructure, including regular security hardening, vulnerability assessments, and configuration management to maintain a secure environment.

Separate Production Environment

Application development activity is separated from the production and test environments. The extent of separation, logical or physical, is appropriate to the risk of the business application or customer contractual requirements. Production data is not used in non-production environments.

Network Security

DMARC

Vizcom has implemented SPF, DKIM, and DMARC protocols in their messaging service to enhance email authentication and prevent email spoofing. Implementing these protocols helps verify the authenticity of emails, increasing trust and reducing the risk of phishing and spoofing attacks.

Firewall

Vizcom ensures that firewall rules are restrictive, specifying source and destination IP addresses, protocols, and other relevant parameters. Restrictive firewall rules help limit access to only necessary and authorized connections, reducing the attack surface and enhancing network security.

IDS/IPS

Vizcom has deployed IDS and IPS devices that are properly configured to enable and monitor network flows intrusion Detection System (IDS) and Intrusion Prevention System (IPS) devices play a crucial role in detecting and preventing unauthorized access and malicious activities within the network

Traffic Filtering

Network traffic is filtered based on defined rules to control and secure data flow, limiting access to necessary ports and protocols only.

Virtual Private Cloud (VPC)

Utilized to create isolated and secure network environments within our cloud providers, providing an additional layer of security and control.
